The idea.

Most companies hire to fill seats they've already shaped. We don't. Some of our best hires were built around the person -- their stack, their instincts, their angle on the GTM problem. The job title came afterwards.

If you read our open roles and don't see yourself in any of them, but you're sure you can build something we don't have yet, this application is for you. Tell us what you'd own, why it matters, and what you'd do in your first 30 days.

What this isn't.

This isn't a generic "join our talent pool" form. We read every submission. The bar is the same as a posted role: be specific, be concrete, and show your work.

Who tends to fit.

  • Operators who have built their own seat inside another company before -- the title on LinkedIn doesn't quite match what you actually do day-to-day.
  • Builders with a stack that crosses categories: copy + automation, ops + design, engineering + GTM strategy, etc.
  • People sitting on a wedge they want to own -- a vertical, a workflow, an underserved client segment, a tool integration we haven't shipped.
  • Generalists who can prove their range with receipts (work, screenshots, repos, campaigns, decks).

How to pitch it.

The form below asks for the basics -- who you are, what you'd own, and why. A few prompts to think about before you fill it in.

  • What problem at Clay Stan would you own? Be specific. "Improve outbound" is not a pitch. "Build the n8n + Clay layer that auto-triggers campaigns from funding announcements" is.
  • What's your unfair advantage? The skill, network, or experience that makes this role obvious for you and not for the next candidate.
  • What does month one look like? If we said yes tomorrow, what would you ship in the first 30 days?
  • What's the receipt? Link us to something you've built, written, or shipped. Public is best, private with a quick walkthrough also works.

We read every application and respond to shortlisted candidates within one week.
