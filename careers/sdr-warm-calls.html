The setup.

Clay Stan runs multi-channel outbound for B2B and venture-backed clients. The campaigns generate warm replies every day -- founders, partners, decision-makers raising their hand. Speed-to-lead is the difference between a booked meeting and a cold trail.

That's where you come in. We've built the automations that pull qualified phone numbers the moment a lead engages. Your job is the human side: pick up the phone, sound like a person, and convert that warm signal into a calendar invite.

What you'll do.

  • Hit warm leads inside a 15-minute window. When a positive reply comes in, you're the first response. Email if needed -- but the phone is the priority.
  • Qualify on the call. Confirm fit, surface intent, handle the basic objections, and route them to the right next step.
  • Book qualified meetings. The metric is calendar invites that show up, not dials made or voicemails left.
  • Run the inbox alongside the dial. Some leads won't pick up -- you'll work email follow-ups in parallel and stay on the thread until they're booked or out.
  • Log everything cleanly. CRM hygiene matters. Notes, dispositions, next steps -- so the rest of the team can pick up where you left off.
  • Feed signal back upstream. If a campaign is producing junk warm leads, you tell us. The GTM Engineer fixes the targeting; you fix the conversation.

What we're looking for.

  • A history of cold calls and outbound dials. You've done the harder version of this job. Warm calls will feel like an upgrade, not a learning curve.
  • An American or British accent -- or the polish of one. Our clients sell into US and UK markets and the call has to land in 5 seconds.
  • Confidence on the phone. Calm, conversational, no script-reading energy. You can think on your feet through a prospect's curveball.
  • Speed-to-lead instincts. When a warm reply hits, your reflex is to dial -- not to put it on a queue for tomorrow.
  • Clean written follow-up. Short, direct, no agency filler. The follow-up email is the second-best touch after the call.
  • Comfort in a CRM. HubSpot, Salesforce, Attio, or similar -- you can manage your own pipeline without supervision.

Big plus

  • You've worked SDR seats inside US-based B2B SaaS, agencies, or venture firms.
  • You've used dialers like CloudTalk, Aircall, JustCall, or Orum.
  • You've sold into founders, partners, or senior operators -- the audience we're calling is rarely line-level.

What this role is not.

This is not a cold-call seat. You're not building lists, scraping numbers, or grinding through 200 dials a day to surface a maybe. The pipeline is warm -- the bar is on conversion, not volume. If you came up doing cold dials and you're tired of fighting for attention, this is the seat where your reps finally pay off.
